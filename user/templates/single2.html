Forex Solutions for SMEs

By Colombus Expert  -  February 01, 2024

Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) often face challenges when it comes to foreign exchange (forex) transactions. Traditional FX providers have extensive offers for large-scale clients but very few bespoke offers for SMEs. As a result, such companies struggle to accept payments from abroad. However, new solutions are emerging on the market, which means that this situation is likely to change. In this article, we will explore the current foreign exchange provision for SMEs, the challenges they face, and the emerging foreign exchange services that can help them overcome these challenges.

Current Foreign Exchange Provisions for SMEs:

The traditional banks tend not to adequately support SMEs, and this comes down to three key points. Firstly, extensive regulatory red tape prevents companies from accessing services such as underwriting or invoice financing. Secondly, insufficient Forex risk advice is provided to SMEs, which results in reduced earnings due to unhedged Forex risk. Lastly, there is an unaffordable one-size-fits-all approach to forex, and there’s still no global payments provision for SME clients.

How do the majority of SMEs currently manage their foreign payments?

SMEs have no choice but to use the same solutions as large corporations and, as a result, have to pay high international transaction fees. Payment forms remain complex and difficult to fill out for the average SME owner or entrepreneur. Once the payment is made, it's often hard to assess when it will reach the final recipient, and such payments can be slow-moving, which can be frustrating in a fast-paced business environment.

Emerging Foreign Exchange Services for SMEs:

The foreign exchange landscape is changing for SMEs due to the emergence of a range of new foreign exchange brokers that are offering to educate SME executives on FX risks and can help prepare them for how to budget FX costs into their quarterly and yearly cash flow forecasts. Companies offering such services include global risk management and payments platforms AFEX and XCAP, which offer SMEs considerable savings on transferring and receiving currency from abroad.

New BaaS Platforms Offering Foreign Currency Services to SMEs:

There has also been a new wave of Banking as a Service (BaaS) solutions for FX, which forms part of the embedded finance revolution and may be particularly suited to SMEs. OpenPayd, Curve, Stripe, Airwallex, Kantox, and TreasurUp are some of the companies that offer such services. These platforms offer SMEs holistic payment solutions that cover all their needs, use the latest AI technology to simplify banking processes for users, have inbuilt risk-management functionality, and offer human support where needed.

What Would an Ideal-World FX Platform for SMEs Look Like?

An ideal-world SME platform would serve the needs of SME leaders who are time-poor, often travel for business purposes, and still tend to favor face-to-face interaction. They would be looking for integrated services that offer them holistic payment solutions that cover all their needs, a solution that uses the latest AI technology to simplify banking processes for users, a platform with instant recognizability wherever they happen to be in the world, a tool that has inbuilt risk-management functionality, human support where needed, a tool that has a real-time overview of the market, and a currency fluctuation monitor.

FX Risk Management:

One of the biggest questions is how to create an advisory tool for FX transactions, which would be a game-changer for customers. A tool to define the risk profile and propose the hedging scenarios for each customer, enabling them to manage their payments and investments more wisely. It is essential to create some intelligent inbuilt patterns for this and distribute them throughout the application. The ultimate goal is to support each client in defining their risk management strategy.

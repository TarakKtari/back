Optimizing Investments Through Currency Hedging:

A Strategic Approach for Asset Managers and Owners

By Colombus Expert  -  February 01, 2024

Currency hedging has become an essential tool for asset managers and owners seeking to optimize their investments in the global market. As central bank policies diverge and foreign exchange (FX) volatility increases, the need to manage currency risk has become increasingly important. In this article, we will explore the various currency hedging techniques and solutions available to asset managers and owners and how these strategies can be leveraged to achieve greater efficiency and risk management in their investment portfolios.

Outsourcing Currency Risk Management: A Strategic Consideration:

Currency hedging is a method used by fund managers to reduce exposure to fluctuations in currency exchange rates. It is similar to insurance, which you buy to protect yourself from an unforeseen event. Currency hedging is an attempt to reduce the effects of currency fluctuations on investment returns. Traditionally, asset managers have launched 'hedged share classes' in locally denominated currencies to attract foreign investors. They can implement a passive hedging strategy, enabling the investor to invest in their own local currency while mitigating FX fluctuations. Investing in international assets can also result in foreign currency exposure. However, as regulatory scrutiny and foreign exchange volatility increase, many asset owners and managers are reevaluating their currency management processes. Outsourcing currency risk management has become a topic of discussion, and it raises important questions about exposure and management, strategy application, execution, and governance and regulatory duties.

Currency management involves four key questions:

What is my currency exposure on a particular date?

kind of strategy should I apply to my exposure?

do I execute my strategy?

do I fulfill my governance and regulatory duties?

Some asset managers may not consider currency performance important, but currency hedging is an important aspect of business for many clients. Dynamic hedging strategies adjust the hedge ratio to achieve a predefined result, while passive strategies rebalance the hedge to a predefined benchmark. Clients often engage in currency hedging to mitigate risk, but they may not be familiar with the process.

Currency Hedging Solutions for Asset Managers and Owners:

Share Class Hedging (SCH) is a strategy designed to mitigate the impact of exchange rate movements between a fund's base currency and the currency of its denomination. It involves using forward forex contracts and adjustments at subscription and redemption, at a predefined exchange rate, and at a defined time period. This strategy allows asset managers to offer the performance of their fund in a specific currency, providing a transparent performance for investors. Multiple

Currency Hedging (MCH) is a combination of portfolio hedging and share class hedging. It allows asset managers to simultaneously hedge each currency bucket of a fund against foreign exchange variation and offer it in currencies different from its base currency. These solutions offer asset managers and owners the flexibility to manage currency risk and offer their funds in different currencies, while minimizing capital requirements and providing transparent performance for investors. These solutions can also include reporting, such as currency allocation reports, pre-trade reports, post-trade reports, hedge change reports, and deal blotters. These solutions are offered by various financial institutions, such as Societe Generale Securities Services, RBC Investor and Treasury Services, Pictet Asset Management, RL360, and J.P. Morgan Asset Management.

RBC Investor and Treasury Services' Perspective on FX Hedging:

FX hedging can have a significant impact on the performance of an asset management product. Share-class hedging aims to mitigate the effect of exchange rate movements between a fund's base currency and the currency of its denomination. Previously, many asset management companies bought an FX share-class hedging service as part of a bundled offering. However, there is a growing trend towards purchasing standalone solutions, with asset managers running dedicated RFP processes to select the best provider in the market. This approach ensures that the performance of the share class mirrors that of the fund's base currency, providing greater transparency and risk management for investors.

Fintech's Role in Currency Hedging for Businesses:

Fintech firms have played a significant role in innovating currency hedging solutions, particularly for businesses with global operations. These solutions integrate hedging facilities with other services such as payments, cashflow forecasting tools, and risk management. By offering multi-currency accounts and integrated platforms, fintech firms have enabled businesses to make informed hedging decisions and avoid significant losses resulting from FX volatility. The urgency for businesses to hedge remains, particularly as currency markets remain volatile, and fintech firms have provided alternative approaches to help businesses manage their currency risk effectively.
